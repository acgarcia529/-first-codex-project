# Executive Summary — Ireland + Scotland Family Trip

## The Trip in One Sentence
Fly from the U.S. to Dublin, immediately continue to Edinburgh for two relaxed Scotland nights, fly back to Belfast, then road-trip Ireland west-to-south-to-east with one castle night and a final easy Dublin Airport night before the locked June 3, 2026 11:00 AM flight.

## Final Route I Would Share With the Family

| Dates | Nights | Base | Why this base makes sense |
|---|---:|---|---|
| May 24 | 0 | Overnight flight | Travel day from the U.S. to Dublin. |
| May 25-27 | 2 | Edinburgh | Scotland is front-loaded; no rental car; easy walkable city sightseeing after the long flight. |
| May 27-28 | 1 | Belfast | Fly Edinburgh → Belfast if price/timing works; pick up the rental car only after Scotland. |
| May 28-29 | 1 | Galway/Salthill | Longest transfer day, but it positions the family for Galway atmosphere and the Cliffs route. |
| May 29-30 | 1 | Dromoland Castle / County Clare | Best geographic castle night between West Ireland and Kerry. |
| May 30-June 1 | 2 | Killarney | Best base for Killarney National Park and a Dingle Peninsula day. |
| June 1-2 | 1 | Kilkenny | Breaks up the Kerry-to-Dublin return and adds Rock of Cashel / medieval Kilkenny. |
| June 2-3 | 1 | Dublin Airport area | Protects the June 3 11:00 AM Dublin Airport departure. |

## Final Transportation Recommendation
- **Book flights:** Dublin → Edinburgh on May 25 after landing, then Edinburgh → Belfast City on May 27 if timing/pricing works.
- **Compare:** Belfast International only if it is meaningfully cheaper or better timed.
- **Fallback:** Edinburgh → Dublin only if Belfast pickup / Dublin Airport drop-off rental fees are unreasonable.
- **Rental car:** pick up in Belfast only after returning from Scotland; return at Dublin Airport on June 2 evening if possible.
- **Verify before paying:** one-way fee, cross-border permission/fee, automatic 7-seater luggage capacity, and insurance.

## What We Are Intentionally Skipping
- **Giant's Causeway:** too much north-coast detour before the Belfast-to-Galway transfer.
- **Connemara/Kylemore Abbey:** beautiful, but it creates backtracking before the Clare/Kerry portion.
- **Full Ring of Kerry:** more all-day driving than this trip needs; Dingle Peninsula is better for teens, beaches, photos, shops, and food.
- **Black Cab tour as a must-do:** meaningful but too much on the same Belfast afternoon as flights, rental car pickup, and Titanic Belfast. Keep it as an optional swap for Titanic if the family prefers history over Titanic.

## Teen-Friendly Highlights
- Edinburgh Castle, Victoria Street, Camera Obscura, Chocolatarium, Calton Hill photos.
- Titanic Belfast, Cathedral Quarter street art, relaxed Belfast dinner.
- Salthill Prom, Galway Latin Quarter, sweets/cafés, buskers if energy allows.
- Cliffs of Moher, Dunguaire Castle photo stop, Burren scenery.
- Dromoland Castle grounds and a special castle-night dinner.
- Killarney National Park, Ross Castle, Torc Waterfall, Inch Beach, Dingle town, Slea Head viewpoints, Murphy's Ice Cream.
- Rock of Cashel, Kilkenny Castle gardens, Butter Slip, Cakeface pastries.
- Dublin Grafton Street / Powerscourt Townhouse / George's Street Arcade before the airport hotel.

## Driving Reality Check
Three days are long-drive days and should stay lightly scheduled:
1. **May 28:** Belfast → Galway/Salthill, about 4.5-5.25 hours plus stops.
2. **May 31:** Killarney → Dingle Peninsula → Killarney, about 4.0-4.75 hours total.
3. **June 1:** Killarney → Rock of Cashel → Kilkenny, about 3.75-4.25 hours plus stops.

## What I Would Personally Book
1. **Flights:** DUB → EDI on May 25 with a generous post-arrival buffer; EDI → Belfast City on May 27 if the flight is well-timed and reasonably priced.
2. **Car:** automatic 7-seat vehicle from Belfast City Airport to Dublin Airport, returned June 2 evening.
3. **Lodging:** central Edinburgh, Belfast Cathedral/Titanic Quarter, Salthill or Galway West End, Dromoland Castle, Killarney town/Muckross Road, central Kilkenny, Dublin Airport hotel.
4. **Castle:** Dromoland Castle first choice; Glenlo Abbey and Ballyseede Castle as backups.
5. **Priority activities:** Edinburgh Castle, Camera Obscura, Chocolatarium, Titanic Belfast, Cliffs of Moher, Rock of Cashel.
6. **Best food picks:** Howies or Vittoria in Edinburgh; Home or Made in Belfast; Ard Bia or John Keogh's in Galway; Dromoland dining for castle night; Bricín or Treyvaud's in Killarney; Petronella or Rive Gauche in Kilkenny; Old Street Malahide or hotel dining near Dublin Airport.
