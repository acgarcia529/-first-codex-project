# Ireland + Scotland Family Itinerary — Hour-by-Hour

Planning note: times are practical placeholders, not flight/attraction reservations. Verify all May/June 2026 flight schedules, opening times, ticket requirements, and restaurant availability before booking.

## Quick Route Verdict

**Recommended route:** DUB → EDI on May 25, 2 nights Edinburgh, EDI → Belfast City preferred on May 27, pick up the rental car only after landing back in Ireland, then road trip Belfast → Galway/Salthill → Dromoland Castle → Killarney/Dingle → Kilkenny/Rock of Cashel → Dublin Airport. This keeps Scotland at the front, avoids a car before Scotland, places the castle night naturally between West Ireland and Kerry, and protects the June 3 11:00 AM Dublin Airport return.

**Planner tightening made in this final version:** Belfast is no longer overloaded with both Titanic Belfast and a Black Cab tour on the same travel afternoon. The final recommendation is Titanic Belfast plus a relaxed Cathedral Quarter evening; a Black Cab tour becomes an optional swap only if the family strongly prefers political history and the flight lands early. Giant's Causeway and Connemara are deliberately left out because they create inefficient backtracking on this compressed itinerary.

## Edinburgh — May 25-27, 2 nights

### Marriott options
- **W Edinburgh / St James Quarter** — best if budget allows; walkable New Town shopping, tram access, and easy taxis to Old Town. Parking is difficult/expensive; do not bring a car. Source: https://www.marriott.com/
- **Courtyard Edinburgh** — practical Marriott-family option near New Town/Calton Hill; confirm family room configuration. Source: https://www.marriott.com/
- **Moxy Edinburgh Fountainbridge** — fun teen-friendly vibe, often better value; longer walk/taxi to Castle/Royal Mile. Source: https://www.marriott.com/

### Aparthotel options
- **Native Edinburgh** — New Town apartment-style base; verify sleeps-5 units. Source: https://nativeplaces.com/properties/native-edinburgh/
- **Staycity Aparthotels Edinburgh** — kitchen/laundry style can help families; verify exact location and 5-person units. Source: https://www.staycity.com/edinburgh
- **Fraser Suites Edinburgh** — Old Town location; verify larger rooms/suites. Source: https://www.frasershospitality.com/

### Airbnb/Vrbo search guidance
Search **Old Town near Grassmarket/Victoria Street** or **New Town near St Andrew Square/George Street**. This reduces taxi time because the itinerary clusters around Castle, Royal Mile, Camera Obscura, Chocolatarium, Princes Street, and Calton Hill. Filters: entire home/apartment, sleeps 5, 2+ bedrooms, ideally 2 bathrooms, washer/dryer, elevator if upper floor, strong recent reviews, flexible cancellation, no car parking required. Parking in central Edinburgh is difficult.

# Driving Summary and Route Logic

## Professional Planner Verdict
This route is geographically sound for the user's fixed constraints: Scotland first, fly back into Belfast, start the rental-car trip only after Scotland, move west to Galway, turn the Cliffs/Clare segment into a castle night, spend two nights in Killarney, then use Kilkenny/Rock of Cashel to break the return to Dublin. The trip is still fast-paced because it covers Scotland, Northern Ireland, West Ireland, Clare, Kerry, Kilkenny, and Dublin Airport in 10 calendar days, so the final version removes non-essential detours and keeps the long-drive days simple.

## Rental Car Plan
- **Do not pick up a car until after Scotland.** Use flights/taxis/tram in Dublin and Edinburgh.
- **Preferred pickup:** Belfast City Airport on May 27 after the Edinburgh flight.
- **Compare pickup:** Belfast International if the better Edinburgh flight lands there.
- **Preferred return:** Dublin Airport on June 2 evening before the airport-hotel night. If returning June 3 morning, allow extra time.
- **Verify before booking:** one-way fee, cross-border Northern Ireland/Republic of Ireland fee, insurance validity, automatic transmission, 7-seat luggage capacity, and late/after-hours return procedure.

## Flight Routing Logic
- **May 25:** Fly Dublin to Edinburgh after the transatlantic arrival. Choose a relaxed afternoon/evening flight so the family is not rushed after immigration and baggage.
- **May 27:** Fly Edinburgh back to Ireland. Belfast City is preferred because it positions the family for Northern Ireland and the westbound road trip. Belfast International is the comparison option. Dublin is a fallback only if Belfast rental economics fail.
- **No ferry/coach:** not recommended with this family/timeframe unless flights are unavailable or unreasonable.

## Lodging Move Logic
- **Edinburgh 2 nights:** keeps Scotland at the front and avoids a car in the city.
- **Belfast 1 night:** necessary to use the preferred Edinburgh-to-Belfast return and start the road trip in Northern Ireland.
- **Galway/Salthill 1 night:** long transfer, but it sets up a natural Cliffs/Clare castle day.
- **County Clare castle 1 night:** best geographic placement between Galway/Cliffs and Kerry.
- **Killarney 2 nights:** prevents a one-night Kerry rush and supports a Dingle day trip.
- **Kilkenny 1 night:** breaks up the Kerry-to-Dublin return and adds Rock of Cashel without threatening departure morning.
- **Dublin Airport 1 night:** protects the locked June 3 11:00 AM flight.

## Backtracking / Inefficiency Risks Removed or Controlled
1. **Giant's Causeway:** removed from the plan; it is too much north-coast detour before a westbound Galway transfer.
2. **Black Cab plus Titanic on May 27:** changed to a choice/swap; doing both after a flight and rental pickup feels rushed.
3. **Connemara/Kylemore Abbey:** left out because Cliffs of Moher connects better to the Clare castle night and Kerry.
4. **Flying EDI back to Dublin:** kept as fallback only; it creates unnecessary backtracking unless Belfast car fees are unreasonable.
5. **Full Ring of Kerry:** not recommended; Dingle is more compact, scenic, colorful, and teen-friendly.
